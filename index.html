Banki is a town and a Subdivision, Legislative Council constituency no. 88 and Notified Area Council in Cuttack district in the state of Odisha, India. Banki is known for the Charchika Temple, dedicated to the deity of Chamunda, the eight armed goddess and incarnation of Durga. In the 2024 Odisha Assembly elections current MLA of Banki is Devi Ranjan Tripathy. Banki is a historic town and a Notified Area Council in the Cuttack district of Odisha, India, nestled on the southern bank of the Mahanadi river. With a rich heritage as a former zamindari state, Banki is renowned for its religious significance, natural beauty, and vibrant local economy. History and political legacy Former princely state: Originating as a zamindari state in the 17th century, Banki was initially ruled by a powerful Raja. The name is believed to derive from Bakra Durg, or "round fort," referring to the shape of the king's fortification. A queen's revenge: The town's legacy includes the tale of Rani Sukadei. After her husband, the king of Banki, was killed in a battle with the king of Khurda, she led her troops to victory, annexed parts of Khurda, and took the title of Maharani. British annexation: The British took control of Banki in 1840 after the last Raja was deposed following a murder conviction. The state was annexed and placed under the direct control of the Bengal Presidency. Modern political center: Today, Banki functions as a subdivision and legislative constituency within the Cuttack district. The town itself is governed by a Notified Area Council (NAC). Geography and climate Riverine setting: Banki is situated at coordinates 20.38¬∞N 85.53¬∞E, on the southern bank of the Mahanadi river, which forms its northern boundary. This location makes fishing an important local activity, with the town famous for its illish fish caught in the river. Tropical climate: The town experiences a tropical wet and dry climate. Summers are hot, with temperatures reaching up to 43¬∞C, while winters are mild. The monsoon season, from June to September, brings moderate to heavy rainfall. Location: Banki is a strategic hub, approximately 58 km from the state capital, Bhubaneswar, and 47 km from the district headquarters in Cuttack. It is surrounded by numerous semi-urban villages that contribute to the town's local economy. Cultural and religious life Charchika Temple: Banki is most famous for the Maa Charchika Temple, a prominent Shakti place of worship. The temple, dedicated to the eight-armed goddess Chamunda, is situated on a small hillock called Ruchika Parvata. The deity is believed to have been placed there by the mythological figure Lord Parshuram. Other religious sites: Numerous other temples and places of worship dot the Banki landscape, including the Shri Mahima Mani Temple, Shri Singhanatha Temple, and the Maa Ratneswari temple in the village of Harirajpur. Local cuisine and handicrafts: The town's cuisine reflects typical Odia preferences, with rice, lentils, and local seafood from the Mahanadi being staples. The Prasad at the Charchika Temple is famously made with fish. Banki is also known for its handicrafts, particularly the Chandua work, involving intricate appliqu√© on fabrics like sarees and dresses. Economy and education Traditional livelihood: The local economy is largely supported by agriculture in the surrounding villages and the town's market, which serves as a trade hub. Fishing in the Mahanadi is another traditional practice. Educational hub: The town is a notable educational center, particularly with the presence of the autonomous Banki College. Established in 1961, the college offers a range of undergraduate and postgraduate courses and provides quality education to students from Banki and the surrounding areas. Demographics Population: According to the 2011 census, Banki had a population of 17,521. Literacy: The town boasts a high average literacy rate of 82%, which is higher than the national average. Religion: The vast majority of the population follows Hinduism, with a smaller presence of other religions like Islam and Christianity.

Mahanadiüö§

The Mahanadi is a major river flowing through the Indian states of Chhattisgarh and Odisha, forming a fertile delta that empties into the Bay of Bengal. Originating in Chhattisgarh, it is known for its high silt-depositing capacity, creating fertile lands for agriculture and supporting significant industries like steel and aluminum in its basin. Key features include the long Hirakud Dam in Odisha, the ancient trading cities of Cuttack and Sambalpur, and its importance as a lifeline for the region's economy and agriculture.

Ansupa Lakeüèû

Ansupa Lake is a large, horseshoe-shaped freshwater lake in Cuttack, Odisha, known for its scenic beauty, diverse biodiversity including migratory birds, and as an important Ramsar site since 2021. It is surrounded by the Saranda Hills and lush greenery of bamboo and mango trees, offering activities like paddle boating, fishing, and picnicking, as well as accommodation at a nature camp managed by Odisha Tourism.

Charchika Templeüõï

The Charchika Temple in Banki, Odisha, is an ancient Hindu temple dedicated to the goddess Charchika, a form of Durga, and is considered one of India's oldest Shakti Peeths. Located on Ruchika Parvata hillock by the Mahanadi River, it houses an eight-armed idol of the goddess. The temple is a significant pilgrimage site, especially during Durgapuja, and attracts many devotees and tourists for its religious significance and serene setting.

devidwaraüõï

Debidwar, also known as Devi Dwar or Devidwar, is a scenic and peaceful riverside tourist spot located near Banki in Cuttack district, Odisha, known for the Maa Ramchandi Temple on the banks of the Mahanadi River. It is a popular destination for nature lovers, offering beautiful views of the hills and river, and is ideal for weekend outings and picnics, especially during the monsoon season. The temple is associated with the Treta Yuga and is visited by devotees during Dussehra, its main festival.

Banki College (Autonomous)üè´

Banki College (Autonomous) is a government college located in Banki, Cuttack, Odisha, founded in 1961 to serve the educational needs of the local rural population. It is affiliated with Utkal University and offers a broad range of courses in Arts, Science, and Commerce, focusing on the holistic development of its students with a commitment to quality education and character building.
